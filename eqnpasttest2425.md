### **1. Find the equation of the circle passing through $(-2, 0)$, $(1, 1)$, and $(-3, 3)$.**

Let the general equation of the circle be $x^2 + y^2 + Dx + Ey + F = 0$.
Substitute the given three points into the equation:
1. For $(-2, 0)$: $(-2)^2 + 0^2 + D(-2) + E(0) + F = 0 \Rightarrow 4 - 2D + F = 0 \Rightarrow 2D - F = 4$ --- (1)
2. For $(1, 1)$: $1^2 + 1^2 + D(1) + E(1) + F = 0 \Rightarrow 2 + D + E + F = 0 \Rightarrow D + E + F = -2$ --- (2)
3. For $(-3, 3)$: $(-3)^2 + 3^2 + D(-3) + E(3) + F = 0 \Rightarrow 18 - 3D + 3E + F = 0 \Rightarrow 3D - 3E - F = 18$ --- (3)

From (1), we have $F = 2D - 4$.
Substitute $F$ into (2): $D + E + (2D - 4) = -2 \Rightarrow 3D + E = 2 \Rightarrow E = 2 - 3D$ --- (4)
Substitute $F$ and $E$ into (3): $3D - 3(2 - 3D) - (2D - 4) = 18 \Rightarrow 3D - 6 + 9D - 2D + 4 = 18 \Rightarrow 10D = 20 \Rightarrow D = 2$.
Using (4): $E = 2 - 3(2) = -4$.
Using (1): $F = 2(2) - 4 = 0$.
The equation of the circle is **$x^2 + y^2 + 2x - 4y = 0$**.

### **2. A circle touches $y$-axis at $A(0, 7)$ and passes through $B(8, 3)$. Find the equation of the circle.**

Since the circle touches the $y$-axis at $(0, 7)$, its center $G$ must lie on the horizontal line $y = 7$.
Let the center be $G(r, 7)$ where $r$ is the radius. Since $B(8, 3)$ is to the right of the $y$-axis, $r > 0$.
The distance from center $G(r, 7)$ to point $B(8, 3)$ is equal to the radius $r$:
$\sqrt{(r - 8)^2 + (7 - 3)^2} = r$
$(r - 8)^2 + 4^2 = r^2$
$r^2 - 16r + 64 + 16 = r^2 \Rightarrow 16r = 80 \Rightarrow r = 5$.
The center is $G(5, 7)$ and radius is $5$.
Equation: $(x - 5)^2 + (y - 7)^2 = 5^2 \Rightarrow$ **$x^2 + y^2 - 10x - 14y + 49 = 0$**.

### **3. Equations of circles $C_1$ and $C_2$ are $3(x - 2)^2 + 3(y - 1)^2 = 108$ and $2x^2 + 2y^2 + 40x + 32y + 166 = 0$ respectively.**

**(a)** $C_1: (x - 2)^2 + (y - 1)^2 = \frac{108}{3} = 36$.
The coordinates of center $G_1$ are **$(2, 1)$** and the radius is **$6$**.

**(b)** $C_2: x^2 + y^2 + 20x + 16y + 83 = 0$.
The coordinates of center $G_2$ are $(\frac{-20}{2}, \frac{-16}{2}) =$ **$(-10, -8)$**.
The radius is $\sqrt{(-10)^2 + (-8)^2 - 83} = \sqrt{100 + 64 - 83} = \sqrt{81} =$ **$9$**.

**(c)** Distance between centers $d = \sqrt{(-10 - 2)^2 + (-8 - 1)^2} = \sqrt{(-12)^2 + (-9)^2} = \sqrt{144 + 81} = 15$.
Sum of radii $r_1 + r_2 = 6 + 9 = 15$.
Since the distance between centers equals the sum of radii ($d = r_1 + r_2$), the two circles touch each other externally at exactly one point. Therefore, **$C_1$ does not intersect $C_2$ at two distinct points**.

### **4. Centre of circle $C$ is $(5, -4)$, $x$-axis is tangent to $C$.**

**(a)** The radius is the distance from $(5, -4)$ to the $x$-axis ($y = 0$), which is $|-4| = 4$.
Equation: $(x - 5)^2 + (y + 4)^2 = 4^2 \Rightarrow$ **$(x - 5)^2 + (y + 4)^2 = 16$**.

**(b)** Line $L: y = x + k$. Substitute into the circle equation:
$(x - 5)^2 + (x + k + 4)^2 = 16 \Rightarrow 2x^2 + (2k - 2)x + (k + 4)^2 + 9 = 0$.
Let $A(x_1, y_1)$ and $B(x_2, y_2)$ be the points of intersection.
The $x$-coordinate of the mid-point is $x_M = \frac{x_1 + x_2}{2} = \frac{-b}{2a} = \frac{-(2k - 2)}{2(2)} = \frac{1 - k}{2}$.
The $y$-coordinate is $y_M = x_M + k = \frac{1 - k}{2} + k = \frac{1 + k}{2}$.
Coordinates of the mid-point are **$(\frac{1 - k}{2}, \frac{1 + k}{2})$**.

### **5. Center $G(h, k)$ lies on $L_1: 3x - 4y - 26 = 0$. $L_2$ is parallel to $L_1$ and touches circle $C$ at $A(3, 2)$. Show $4h + 3k = 18$.**

Since $L_2$ is parallel to $L_1$, its slope is the same: $m = \frac{3}{4}$.
Line $GA$ is the radius connecting the center to the point of tangency, so it is perpendicular to $L_2$.
Slope of $GA = \frac{-1}{3/4} = -\frac{4}{3}$.
$\frac{k - 2}{h - 3} = -\frac{4}{3} \Rightarrow 3(k - 2) = -4(h - 3) \Rightarrow 3k - 6 = -4h + 12 \Rightarrow$ **$4h + 3k = 18$** (Shown).

### **6. Points $E(6, 7)$, $F(3, 13)$, center $G(-2, 3)$. Circle $C$ passes through $E$.**

**(a)** Radius $r = EG = \sqrt{(-2 - 6)^2 + (3 - 7)^2} = \sqrt{(-8)^2 + (-4)^2} = \sqrt{80}$.
Equation: **$(x + 2)^2 + (y - 3)^2 = 80$**.

**(b)** Distance $FG = \sqrt{(-2 - 3)^2 + (3 - 13)^2} = \sqrt{25 + 100} = \sqrt{125} \approx 11.18$.
Radius $r = \sqrt{80} \approx 8.94$.
Since $FG > r$, **point $F$ lies outside the circle $C$**.

**(c) (i)** Farthest distance $FH = FG + r = \sqrt{125} + \sqrt{80} \approx$ **$20.1$** (to 3 sig. fig.).
**(ii)** Max area of $\triangle FGH = \frac{1}{2} \cdot |FG| \cdot r \cdot \sin(90^\circ) = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \sqrt{125} \cdot \sqrt{80} = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \sqrt{10000} =$ **$50$**.

### **7. Circle $C$ center $(-4, 2)$, radius $\sqrt{17}$. Line $L: y = mx$.**

**(a)** Equation: $(x + 4)^2 + (y - 2)^2 = 17 \Rightarrow$ **$x^2 + y^2 + 8x - 4y + 3 = 0$**.

**(b) (i)** Substitute $y = mx$: $(1 + m^2)x^2 + (8 - 4m)x + 3 = 0$.
Product of roots **$x_1x_2 = \frac{3}{1 + m^2}$**.
**(ii)** $OA = |x_1|\sqrt{1 + m^2}$, $OB = |x_2|\sqrt{1 + m^2}$. Since $x_1, x_2 < 0$, $OA \times OB = x_1x_2(1 + m^2)$.
$OA \times OB = (\frac{3}{1 + m^2})(1 + m^2) =$ **$3$**.
